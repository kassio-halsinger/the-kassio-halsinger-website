Completed Projects

A selection of rigorous academic research and technical documentation.

Fourier Series, the Fourier Transform and its applications for the Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle and the Radon Transform

Senior Thesis Research Project | Western Washington University

Why this matters for Data Analysis:

While this research is highly theoretical, the underlying mathematics are the exact mechanics used to clean, parse, and analyze messy real-world data. The Fourier Transform is used to take a complicated, chaotic signal—like years of fluctuating sales history—and break it apart into clear, predictable patterns, allowing analysts to accurately isolate seasonality from base growth.

Similarly, the Radon Transform is the mathematical logic utilized in CAT scans. It demonstrates an advanced ability to take limited fragments of data from multiple different angles and computationally reconstruct a complete, accurate, multi-dimensional picture. This project proves an advanced capability to mathematically isolate signal from noise, a critical skill for building reliable forecasting engines and complex operational models.
